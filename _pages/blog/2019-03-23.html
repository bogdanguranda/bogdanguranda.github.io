OK, so these aren't some random cookies, but are actually specifically made for Purim. They are called "hamentashen" (Haman's pockets, in Yiddish) and אוזני המן (Oznei Haman = Haman's ears, in Hebrew). They are traditionally filled with poppy seed, but nowadays can be filled with raspberry, apricot and who knows what. So, who's this guy, Haman?

The story of Purim

The story of Purim is described in the Tanakh (Jewish Scriptures) in the Book of Esther or מגילת אסתר (Megillat Esther = Scroll of Esther) in Hebrew. Now I've just added another name which I didn't explain, so why not add 2 more: Ahasuerus, the king of Persia (presumed to be Xerxes I or Artaxerxes I of Persia) and last but not least, Esther's uncle Mr. Mordechai. That's it! The plot is simple, like in your favourite super-heroes movie.

There's the villain, Haman, he's going to do some terrible things. And there's also the un-experienced super-hero, Esther, who's got an invaluable power and get's to know the villain before things get crazy. Then there's the neutral character, at first at least, but powerful, who gets corrupted by the evil forces, that would be king Ahasuerus,

and last, but not least, the veteran super-hero, who will recruit the un-experience one and come up with a plan to defeat the evil forces; and that my friends is, the legend himself, uncle Mordechai.

Got it, right? Good.

Now, at stake is עם ישראל (am Israel = nation of Israel), the plan is to use Esther's influence as the new queen, to reverse the evil decree that Haman pushed by using the influence he has over the king. I don't want to go in to details, but after plenty of parties that Ester throws, they are able to pull off the incredible plan and save their people! Hurray! That means another party, right?! Yes! More parties!

Purim in Tel-Aviv

So... now I get to my next point which is: Purim is the happiest holiday of the Jewish people! And in that spirit, Jews worldwide wear masks, dress up in costumes, and party..., but in Tel-Aviv they party... and party... and party... and the amount of costumes is astonishing.

What's happening in this city these days is just incredible, people just dress up as anything... party every night and it doesn't stop after 1 day, it's still lasting as we speak and it's been almost 3 days already.
