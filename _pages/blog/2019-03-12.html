Volunteering in a soup kitchen

Yes, I'm volunteering in Israel (Tel-Aviv) in a soup kitchen! Serving free food to people in need. How about that?! I'm a software engineer, makes total sense. Anyway, let's get in to it!

The organisation is called Lasova, which means something in Hebrew, but apparently even Israelis can't tell what (Israelis please help!). You can find more info about the organisation here: https://www.lasova.org.il/ It has just been my first day, and I had a good experience. There's a hard part, especially if you don't know Hebrew, because people ask you things in Hebrew, but still, they don't ask complicated things, so it's still manageable. Also standing 4 hours on my feet and moving around almost all the time was a bit tiring for the first day. I'm sure some of you do harder stuff, so not gonna complain. But hey, let's look at the up side also! I get to learn some Hebrew and practice it live in real situations. And I get the change to do a good deed helping people, in Israel, which you might want to try, to see how rewarding it is, even if it's not easy (or especially?).

There are other good volunteering opportunities, for example, there's Magen David Adom (Israel's national emergency medical, disaster, ambulance and blood bank service. The name means"Red Star of David"). I have 2 flatmates that are doing this, my roommate is one of them and he's actually on a night shift right now, 11 PM - 7 AM. Now I've scared you!

Ulpan Bayit

Who the hell is Ulpan (אולפן)? It's not a he/she guys and gals, it is a Hebrew word meaning "studio", "teaching", or "instruction". Bayit (בית) is the name of this Ulpan, which means "home".

You got the idea, right? It's a Hebrew language school. It's a unique thing to Israel as far as I can tell and it makes sense as it was needed in order to teach Jewish immigrants (and still needed) from around the globe, the Hebrew language, while making Aliyah (Jews returning to their homeland, Israel). So, besides other things, I've joined this Ulpan to learn Hebrew, and I can tell you that the atmosphere here is really like at home! If you're planning to stay for a longer period in Israel, I believe this is a really useful thing to do as it helps you navigate daily life better.

Enjoying Israel...

There's a lot of ways one can enjoy Israel, and I'm far from trying all of them, yet... But, here's a very few places to visit, that I want to share with you, mostly things that I have photos of... (I'm still bad about doing lots of photos, many times it happens that I just forget to do any, which we can argue is both good and bad).

A good place to visit while in Tel-Aviv, is the city of Yafo. You might see sometimes that Tel-Aviv is called Tel Aviv - Yafo. Yes, that Yafo, they are united, but were 2 separate cities when Tel-Aviv was founded in 1909. Actually, 66 Jewish families from Yafo went to the desert to start building what is today Tel-Aviv. Cool, huh?!

Yafo (Jaffa) — one of the oldest ports in the world, just south of Tel Aviv

It's one of the most ancient ports recorded in history, dating back to ~4000 years ago, and it is believed to be the place where Jonah had set sail, for those of you who know the story from the Tanakh. It's also home to many Arabs, so it's a different experience than Tel-Aviv.

Another place I've found really beautiful it's the old port of Tel-Aviv, even though there was a storm coming, so obviously the place was pretty much empty. But I think that it must be a really amazing place with some vibrant action going on. There's also a lot of restaurants there and the view is great.

You might not know, but from a flora point of view Israel is not as green as most European countries for example, it resembles Greece or Southern Italy more, I think. That means that when you find a park like this you're going to love it!
